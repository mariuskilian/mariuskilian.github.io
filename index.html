About

My journey into Computer Science started at the prestigious KIT in Germany, a member university of the "Excellence Initiative", equipping me with a great theoretical understanding of computer, science principles, programming, architecture and design principles, while also providing a very strong mathematical foundation, with, modules in analysis, linear algebra, numerics, stochastic calculus, combinatorics, graph theory and more.

I then worked for a year as a Software Engineer at the largest telecommunications company in Switzerland, Swisscom, show more... where I lead front-end development for a brand new, innovative service, which consistently impressed our stakeholders, and even landed us a meeting with the head of B2C operations. Further, I also helped with architecture design and back-end development, while being the head of security and reliability of our team. These experiences make me well-equipped in writing easily testable and maintainable code. I greatly enjoyed the challenge and responsibilities that came with these positions, along with the extremely positive environment our team established, allowing everyone to speak their mind, provide ideas, and offer solutions through clear and open communication.

After this year, I turned down an offer to stay, in order to pursue a masters degree in game development at Queen Mary, University of London, which I completed with Distinction in every, module. Game development has always been a passion of mine, as shown by my numerous personal projects. Here, I hoped to combine my creative approaches with my passion for programming and games. The course deepened my knowledge and understanding of hardware-close languages such as C++, while fortifying my creative approach through challenging me with designing games.
